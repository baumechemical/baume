Brass plating deposits a Cu-Zn alloy for a golden tone. Key challenges: adhesion (esp. on steel), uniform color/brightness, and tarnish resistance.

Safety: Some systems use highly hazardous chemistry (e.g., cyanide). Require training, ventilation, never acidify, and keep emergency procedures. Consider non-cyanide systems where appropriate.

TL;DR ‚Äì starter window & checklist

  • Temp/CD: Start mid-spec (often RT‚Äì50 ¬∞C, 0.5‚Äì2.5 A/dm¬≤) and tune one variable at a time.
  • Anodes: Brass or inert with dissolved metals; bag & clean regularly.
  • Flow/filtration: 3‚Äì5√ó/h, 1‚Äì10 ¬µm elements to reduce pitting.
  • Color: Yellow tone rises with Zn in deposit; affected by CD, temp, additives.
  • Post: DI rinses + Anti-tarnish or electro-lacquer.

Surface prep (steel vs copper)

On steel

  • Thorough degrease/clean.
  • Acid activate ‚Üí very thin copper or nickel strike to prevent immersion deposit and boost adhesion.
  • Enter brass main with a current ramp-up.

On copper/brass

  • De-oxidize/desmut ‚Üí DI rinses √ó2‚Äì3.
  • Start at low CD to avoid grainy/dull, then move to mid-range.

Cu/Zn control & bath care

  • Deposit composition (and color) depends on bath metal balance and electrical conditions.
  • Track metals, complexers/conductors, and organic additives; dose by ampere-hours with control charts.
  • Hold pH/temperature steady to reduce color drift.
  • Continuous filtration, clean anode bags, periodic sludge decant.

Anti-tarnish & lacquer

  • Anti-tarnish (cold/hot/electro/tin-co-deposition) per spec‚Äîsee selection guide.
  • Electro-/spray lacquer to lock tone and gloss‚Äîsee lacquer troubleshooting.

QA & maintenance

  • Tests: color/brightness, thickness, adhesion, tarnish/humidity, salt-spray as required.
  • Routine: log Temp/pH/CD/Ah each shift; daily titration & Hull Cell; weekly filters & contacts; monthly full analysis & calibration.
