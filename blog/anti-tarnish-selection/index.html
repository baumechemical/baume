TL;DR ¬∑ Selection criteria ¬∑ Type comparison ¬∑ Process setup ¬∑ Testing/specs ¬∑ Troubleshooting ¬∑ Related

Anti-tarnish films (nano-thin) slow oxidation/sulfidation on Ag, Cu/Brass, Ni and Sn. Pick by substrate, environment, production constraints, and test requirements.

TL;DR ‚Äì quick picks

  • Ag in sulfur-rich air: choose Hot or Electro with sulfide-barrier polymers.
  • Cu/Brass indoors: Cold (BTA + polymer) = economical & easy.
  • Frequent touch/sweat: Hot with thermal set.
  • Later soldering: Electro or solderable formulations.
  • Sn surfaces: Tin anti-tarnish / immersion tin to prevent white haze.

Selection criteria

  1. Substrate (Ag/Cu-Brass/Ni/Sn).
  2. Environment: humidity 40‚Äì95%RH, sulfur (rubber/leather), marine salt, handling.
  3. Production: hot capability, DI quality, rectifier for Electro, cleanliness.
  4. Tests: humidity/sulfur/salt-spray hours, color ŒîE*, solderability, RoHS.

Process setup

  1. Pre-clean ‚Üí DI rinses ‚Üí light activation (Cu/Brass).
  2. Dip in chosen anti-tarnish under specified temp/time/CD.
  3. DI rinses √ó2‚Äì3, keep drag-in minimal.
  4. Dry/bake as specified (Hot requires thermal set).
  5. Control bath by conductivity/actives; fine filtration.
