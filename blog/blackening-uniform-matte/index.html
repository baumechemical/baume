Blackening (black oxide) forms a very thin black film on steels. Uniform matte and rub resistance depend on prep, a stable time‚Äìtemperature‚Äìchemistry window, and the right sealer.

TL;DR ‚Äì starter settings

  • Bath type: Hot black oxide 135‚Äì145 ¬∞C or room-temp blackening 20‚Äì35 ¬∞C
  • Time: 5‚Äì20 min (hot) ¬∑ 2‚Äì10 min (RT); stop when tone is full & uniform
  • Prep: Degrease ‚Üí DI rinse ‚Üí short acid pickle ‚Üí DI rinse
  • Seal: rust-inhibiting oil / microcrystalline wax / polymer topcoat for rub resistance & salt-spray life
Safety: Hot caustic/oxidizers at high temperature ‚Äî PPE, face shield, ventilation.

Surface preparation

  1. Alkaline/ultrasonic clean to pass the water-break test
  2. Acid pickle briefly to remove oxides/rust; DI rinses √ó2
  3. Matte the base (e.g., fine blast) if a deep matte look is required
  4. Fixture to avoid gas entrapment & ensure uniform flow
