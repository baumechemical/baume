Why sealing Cr¬≥‚Å∫

Trivalent chromate relies on film thickness + sealing/topcoat to reach higher NSS/ASS/CASS hours, control fingerprints/rub, and keep color consistent.

Types of seals/topcoats

  • Inorganic seals: dedicated trivalent sealers, silicate, thin silane/zirconium ‚Äî minimal color shift, good NSS boost.
  • Organic topcoats: acrylic/urethane/wax (water/solvent), dip-spin ‚Äî higher NSS gain, friction control, gloss/matte tuning.
  • Hybrid: inorganic + organic thin layers ‚Äî balanced clarity, rub resistance, and hours.

Indicative durability gain

  • Cr¬≥‚Å∫ clear/blue + inorganic seal ‚Üí ~120‚Äì240 h NSS (typical)
  • Cr¬≥‚Å∫ yellow + inorganic seal ‚Üí ~240‚Äì360 h NSS
  • Cr¬≥‚Å∫ + organic topcoat ‚Üí larger gains; useful for high-hour specs / controlled friction

Actual results depend on Zn thickness, chromate quality, rinsing, drying/curing, and test method.

Starter setup & steps

  1. Post-chromate DI rinses √ó2‚Äì3.
  2. Sealer/topcoat per TDS (typical 20‚Äì90 s, light agitation; dip/spin for organics).
  3. Dry/oven cure as specified to reach rub/friction targets.
  4. Air-knife/spin-dry to avoid runs and water marks.
  5. Verify color, rub, friction (if any), then NSS/humidity tests.
Sequence: If hydrogen bake is required, do it before chromate/seal to avoid film degradation.

Troubleshooting

  • White haze: extra DI rinses, softer water, faster dry/spin.
  • Dull/off-color: correct pH/temp/time; reduce film build; adjust viscosity.
  • Low rub resistance: thicker/topcoat-specific sealer; cure properly.
  • Low NSS: increase sealer time; ensure sufficient Zn & chromate film.
  • Runs/drips: improve drainage; adjust hang angle; air-knife.

QA/QC

  • Salt spray (ASTM B117 / ISO 9227).
  • Friction for fasteners per client spec.
  • Rub/abrasion checks; color vs standard card.
